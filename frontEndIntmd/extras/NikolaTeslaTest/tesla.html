Quick Facts

  • Birthdate: July 10, 1856
  • Birthplace: Smiljan, Austrian Empire
  • Death date: January 7, 1943

Nikola Tesla was a very influential man.

Early Years

  • Tesla was born on 10 July, 1856 into a Serb Family in the Smijan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia)
  • Tesla had a talent for making home craft tools and mechanical appliances; he also had a knack for memorizing epic Serbian poems
  • Tesla became interested in demonstrations of electricity by physics professor
  • Tesla could perform integral calculus in his head, leading to allegations of cheating
  • In 1873, he contracted cholera after returning home in Smijan; he faced near death multiple times
  • His father in despair, promised to send him to the best engineering school if Tesla recovered. Initially, he intended to enter Tesla into priesthood
  • In 1874, Tesla evaded to Tomingaj, near Gracac to avoid conscription; exploring mountains in a hunter’s garb. He claimed the contact with nature made him stronger physically and mentally.
  • In 1875, Tesla enrolled into Austrian Polytechnic in Graz, Austria
  • Tesla initially did well, earning the highest grades, passing more exams than needed, and starting a Serb culture club
  • At the end of his second year, Tesla lost his scholarship and became addicted to gambling. This led to losing his allowance and tuition money.
  • In December 1878, Tesla severed all ties with his family to hide dropping out of school
  • He worked as a draftsman and a teacher from 1878 to 1881 in various locations, suffering a nervous breakdown and the death of his father
  • In 1881, Tesla working at the Budapest Telephone Exchange, made numerous improvements to the Central Station equipment, and perfected the telephone repeater or amplifier
